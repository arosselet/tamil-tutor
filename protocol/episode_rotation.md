# Inspiration (Not Rules)

Optional sparks for the Architect, not a mandatory rotation. The Architect has full creative freedom — this exists only to break writer's block.

---

## Episode Flavors

An episode can take many shapes. A few to pull from:

- **The Eavesdrop** — two hosts overhearing or recounting something from a public place: bus stop, tea stall, market, park.
- **The Dispute** — a disagreement with real stakes: a fare argument, a family standoff, a misunderstanding at a shop.
- **The Transaction** — buying, bargaining, ordering, negotiating.
- **The Gossip** — two hosts riffing about someone or something, with opinion and personality.
- **The Pattern Riff** — a conversation that happens to drill a tense or verb pattern, driven by curiosity rather than instruction.
- **The Debrief** — the hosts unpacking something that "happened" offscreen, like a post-mortem with personality.
- **The Callback Episode** — built primarily around resurfacing struggled/recent vocabulary, with new words as a light accent.

---

## Seeds (Pick One or Invent Your Own)

- A dispute over an autorickshaw fare.
- Overhearing a couple arguing at a chai stall.
- Navigating a crowded vegetable market.
- A tense family dinner with competing opinions.
- A construction site argument about supplies.
- Two shopkeepers competing for the same customer.
- A wedding planning disagreement between in-laws.
- A phone call gone wrong in public.
- A hospital waiting room full of impatient relatives.
- A mechanic explaining — badly — what's wrong with a bike.

---

## The Only Rule

Don't repeat the last episode. If it was domestic, go public. If it was calm, go loud. If it was a scene, try gossip or a debrief. Beyond that — surprise us.
