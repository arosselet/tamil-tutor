# The Immersion Gradient

## Word Categories

Every word in a script falls into one of these three categories, determined by `progress/learner.json`:

| Category | Source | How to Write |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **NEW** (4-5) | Words pulled for this mission by Director | Weave naturally into Intercept dialogue, 3-4x in different contexts. No glossing mid-scene. Fewer words, deeper exposure. |
| **USE** (Mastered) | From `mastered_words` in learner.json | Use freely in Tamil. Never explain or translate. The learner knows these. **These should be the backbone of the Tamil in the Intercept.** |
| **STRUGGLED** (Remedial) | From `struggled_words` in learner.json | Use only if the scene naturally calls for them. Never force. Skip if they don't fit. |

---

## Tier-Specific Tamil Density

This is how much Tamil (vs. English) should appear in each tier:

| Tier | Intercept | Breakdown |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Tier 1** | ~20% Tamil | ~10% Tamil |
| **Tier 2** | ~70% Tamil | ~20% Tamil |
| **Tier 3** | ~100% Tamil | ~50% Tamil |

**Intercept Rule:** Characters use Tamil for emotion, commands, and local color. English for nouns, logistics, filler. This is natural Thanglish.

**Breakdown Rule:** Minimal Tamil. Mostly English analysis with Tamil word quotes/examples.

---

## The Cardinal Rules

1. **NEW words must appear 3-4x in different contexts**, not repeated the same way. Fewer words, more repetition per word.
2. **Never gloss USE words** — they're known, don't explain them.
3. **STRUGGLED words are optional** — use only if they fit the scene naturally.
4. **Never inline-gloss mid-dialogue** — no `word (English)` in Intercept. If context doesn't clarify, the Breakdown handles it.
5. **No textbook Tamil** — sounds like a Coimbatore native speaking, not a textbook being read.
6. **The Comprehensible Input Rule (CRITICAL):** NEVER use a Tamil word in the Intercept that is not in the NEW, USE, or STRUGGLED lists. If the scene requires a word the learner hasn't learned yet, **use English**. Every unexplained Tamil word that isn't in the learner's known vocabulary is noise that makes the episode feel like a wall of sound. The Tamil percentage targets are a ceiling, not a floor — if reaching the target requires unknown Tamil words, lower the percentage and use English. The listener should understand **90%+** of what they hear.

---

## Example: Word Weaving Done Right

**NEW words (4-5 only):**
- தேடு (search) — appears 3-4x: "where are you looking?", "searching for something", "I searched everywhere"
- வாங்கு (buy) — appears 3-4x: "buying vegetables", "want to buy", "already bought"

**USE words — the backbone (never explain):**
- பாக்குறேன் (I'm looking)
- வேணும் (need/want)
- சரி (okay)
- கொஞ்சம் (a little)
- [etc. from mastered list — use as many as fit naturally]

**Everything else: English.** If the scene needs a word like "freshness" or "crowded" and it's not in any list, say it in English. That's natural Thanglish AND it keeps the episode comprehensible.

**STRUGGLED words (if organic):**
- தூக்கு (lift) — if a character picks something up, use it
- Otherwise skip

**Result:** The listener understands 90%+ of the episode. NEW words stand out against a familiar backdrop instead of drowning in a sea of unknown Tamil. Mastered words reinforce confidence. The experience feels like progress, not noise.
