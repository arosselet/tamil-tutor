# Level 4 Episode 2: Past Verbs (Consumption) - (Beat Sheet)

**Target Duration:** 20 Minutes (4 Acts of ~5 mins each)
**Vibe:** Medium Energy ("The Walkman"). Casual conversation, relatable anecdotes, light Thanglish drilling.
**Zinger:** "நல்லா தூங்கினேன்" (I slept well)

## Target Vocabulary
- சாப்பிட்டேன் (I ate)
- குடிச்சேன் (I drank)
- பார்த்தேன் (I saw / I watched)
- தூங்கினேன் (I slept)
- எழுந்தேன் (I woke up)

---

## Act 1: The Setup & The Wake Up (Minutes 0-5)
1. **Intro:** Host welcomes Guest. Recaps the transition into Level 4: The Debrief. "You are no longer reacting in the present; you are reporting the past."
2. **Concept - The "-aen" Suffix:** Explaining how past tense verbs for "I" almost always end with the "-aen" sound. The sonic signature of personal history.
3. **Drill - Waking Up:** Introducing "எழுந்தேன்" (I woke up). Guest practices the sound.
4. **Banter / Story - The Jet Lag:** Guest shares an anecdote about waking up at weird hours and trying to explain it to the house help.
5. **Drill - Sleeping:** The logical pair to waking up. "தூங்கினேன்" (I slept). Host asks "What did you do at 10 PM?" Guest replies "தூங்கினேன்".

## Act 2: Food and Drink Log (Minutes 5-10)
6. **Concept - The Ingestion Verbs:** Moving to the most common morning questions. Food and drink.
7. **Drill - Drinking:** Introducing "குடிச்சேன்" (I drank). Pair it with "Coffee". Guest: "Coffee kudichaen". 
8. **Story - The Filter Coffee Ritual:** Host explains the cultural importance of the morning "Coffee kudichaen" status update.
9. **Drill - Eating:** The mighty "சாப்பிட்டேன்" (I ate). Host throws items: "Dosa". Guest: "Dosa saapitaen". "Idli". Guest: "Idli saapitaen".
10. **Roleplay - The Mother-in-Law Interrogation:** Host asks: Did you eat? (Saaptingala from Level 1). Guest replies "Saapitaen". 
11. **Banter:** Discussing how "Saapitaen" is basically the Tamil equivalent of "I'm doing well, thanks for asking".

## Act 3: Media and Sight (Minutes 10-15)
12. **Concept - Consumption of Vision:** Moving from food to media and sights. Introducing "பார்த்தேன்" (I saw / I watched).
13. **Drill - The Screen Junkie:** Host says "Movie". Guest: "Movie paarthaen". Host says "TV". Guest: "TV paarthaen".
14. **Drill - Real World Sight:** Host says "Auto". Guest: "Auto paarthaen". 
15. **Concept - The Double Verification:** Host asks "Did you see?" (Paartheengala?). Guest replies "Paarthaen". 
16. **Roleplay - The Weekend Debrief:** Host asks what the guest did on Sunday. Guest strings it together: "I woke up. I ate. I watched a movie. I slept."

## Act 4: The Morning Routine Simulation (Minutes 15-20)
17. **Concept - Chronology Intro:** A sneak peek at the next episode. Stringing verbs together without the "and".
18. **Drill - The Sequence (Part 1):** Host: Wake up. Guest: எழுந்தேன். Host: Drink coffee. Guest: Coffee குடிச்சேன்.
19. **Drill - The Sequence (Part 2):** Host: Eat dosa. Guest: Dosa சாப்பிட்டேன். Host: Watch TV. Guest: TV பார்த்தேன்.
20. **Drill - The Sequence (Part 3):** Host: Sleep. Guest: தூங்கினேன்.
21. **Story - The Perfect Day:** Guest reflects on how simple a good day sounds when broken down into just five past-tense verbs.
22. **Drill - Zinger Callback:** Refreshing yesterday's Zinger "ஜாஸ்தி". Host: "I ate 10 idlis." Guest: "பத்து? ஜாஸ்தி!"
23. **Review - The Core Five:** Final rapid-fire validation: Woke up, Slept, Drank, Ate, Watched.
24. **Outro:** Delivering the new threshold Zinger ("நல்லா தூங்கினேன்" - I slept well). Perfect for morning greetings. See you next time.
