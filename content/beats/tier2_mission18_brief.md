# Mission 18 Brief: The Chennai Connection

## Rotation Style: The Transaction
## Location: Chennai Central — Taxi Stand (Arrival)

---

## Intel Target
The learner has just got off the train at Chennai Central. The goal is to get to a specific address (a family home in T. Nagar). This involves navigating the chaos of the taxi stand, dealing with an auto driver who doesn't want to use the meter, and negotiating a fair price. The learner needs to use "must go" commands and verify ability/possibility.

## The Listenability Test
The frantic, sweaty, high-intensity energy of a major Indian transit hub. The specific tension of the "first negotiation" of the day. The dialogue should feel like a volley — fast, semi-hostile but ultimately functional.

---

## Word Status Sheet

### TEACH (The Keys) — 2-3 words
- **போகணும்** → Must go
- **முடியாதா?** → Is it not possible? (The negotiation pivot)
- **எப்படி?** → How? / Which way?

### EXPOSE (The Signal) — 10-12 words
- **பணம்** → Money
- **மீட்டர்** → Meter
- **ஜாஸ்தி** → Too much / High (Price)
- **கம்மி** → Less / Low
- **சீக்கிரம்** → Quickly
- **வழி** → Way / Route
- **தெரியும்** → Know
- **தப்பு** → Wrong / Mistake
- **பண்ணுங்க** → Please do
- **ரூபா** → Rupees

### USE (mastered — no translation)
- சரி, ஆமா, இல்ல, அண்ணா, போறேன், வரேன், எங்க?, என்ன?, இருக்கு, இல்ல, வலது, இடது, நேரா

### CALLBACK (struggled — embedded in scene)
- **முதல்ல** (first) — "First tell me the price."
- **சொல்லுங்க** (please tell)
- **கேட்டேன்** (I asked) — "I already asked that guy."

---

## Narrative Arc

### Intercept Beats (3-5 min, ~700 words)
1. **Ambient open**: Heavy station noise. Porters shouting. Auto engines idling.
2. **The approach**: A driver approaches the learner. "அண்ணா, எங்க போகணும்?" (Where must you go?).
3. **The destination**: Learner (as character) specifies T. Nagar. The driver scoffs at the meter.
4. **The volley**: 500 rupees. "ஜாஸ்தி!" (Too much). "300 முடியாது!" (300 is impossible). The debate over "How?" (எப்படி?). Driver argues traffic is bad.
5. **The resolution**: Negotiating for a mid-point. "முதல்ல சொல்லுங்க..." (First tell me). Agreement at 400.
6. **The departure**: Loading the bag. "சீக்கிரம் போங்க" (Go quickly).

### Breakdown Beats (6-8 min, ~1100 words)
1. **Cold Open Teaser**: "You're about to land in the middle of Chennai Central. Listen for the word `போகணும்`. It sounds like a hammer hitting a nail. It's how you tell the world where you're headed."
2. **Replay Hook**: The moment the driver says "300 முடியாது" (300 is impossible) and the learner counters with "முடியாதா?" (Is it really not possible?).
3. **Unpack போகணும்**: Maya and Raj discuss the 'must' suffix. How it turns a suggestion into a non-negotiable directive.
4. **Unpack முடியாதா?**: The power of the question mark. Turning a rejection back into a negotiation.
5. **Notice the EXPOSE**: Meter, traffic, route. How these Thanglish words help you anchor the Tamil around them.
6. **Drill beats**: "I must go" → போகணும். "Can you do it?" → முடியுமா?.
7. **Zinger**: "ஜாஸ்தி அண்ணா!" — "It's too much, brother!" The ultimate Chennai survival phrase.
