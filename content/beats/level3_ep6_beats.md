# Level 3 Episode 6: Rest and Reps (Beat Sheet)

**Target Duration:** 20 Minutes (4 Acts of ~5 mins each)
**Vibe:** Medium Energy ("The Walkman"). Casual conversation, relatable anecdotes, light Thanglish drilling.
**Zinger:** "ஜாஸ்தி!" (Too much!)

## Target Vocabulary
- வலது (Right)
- இடது (Left)
- எவ்வளவு? (How much?)
- ஜாஸ்தி (Too much)
- ஒன்னு (One)
- பத்து (Ten)

---

## Act 1: The Setup & Spatial Warmup (Minutes 0-5)
1. **Intro:** Casual banter. The host welcomes the guest back. Acknowledges that today is an "Audit Day" - no new words, just solidifying the survival toolkit. Emotional outcome: Relaxed, feeling capable.
2. **Concept - Urban Survival Recap:** The host explains why recalling these words quickly is the difference between a cheap auto ride and getting scammed.
3. **Drill - Left vs Right (Basic):** Focus on "வலது" (Right) and "இடது" (Left). Host gives english, Guest gives Tamil.
4. **Banter / Story - The Confused Auto Driver:** Guest tells a short Thanglish story about accidentally saying "Left" instead of "Right" and ending up in traffic.
5. **Drill - Spatial Speed Round:** Rapid fire with "வலது" and "இடது" but mixed with "நேரா" (Straight) and "நிறுத்துங்க" (Stop) from previous eps.

## Act 2: The Money Foundation (Minutes 5-10)
6. **Concept - The Power of 'How Much':** Transition to commerce. "எவ்வளவு?" (How much?). Why tone matters when asking.
7. **Drill - Price Check:** Host names items (coffee, auto ride), Guest must actively ask "எவ்வளவு?".
8. **Concept - Numbers 1 & 10:** Relooking at "ஒன்னு" (One) and "பத்து" (Ten). Why these are the base anchors.
9. **Drill - Number Combos:** Host: "Give me one." Guest: "ஒன்னு குடுங்க." Host: "Ten rupees." Guest: "பத்து காசு."
10. **Roleplay - The Filter Coffee Stand:** Guest orders 1 coffee, asks how much, is told 10. Simple execution loop.
11. **Banter - The Expat Tax:** Host jokes about how locals know if you are an outsider just by how you say "பத்து".

## Act 3: The Art of Complaining (Minutes 10-15)
12. **Concept - The Scammer Defense:** Introducing "ஜாஸ்தி" (Too much) as the ultimate weapon.
13. **Drill - Outrage Practice:** Host shouts out numbers, Guest must reply with a dramatic "ஜாஸ்தி!".
14. **Story - The 500 Rupee Banana:** A hilarious anecdote about a vendor trying to charge way too much, and the power of walking away.
15. **Drill - Negotiations:** Mixing "எவ்வளவு?", numbers, and "ஜாஸ்தி". Host: "That's 100." Guest: "Nooru? Jaasthi!"
16. **Concept - Returning to Balance:** Acknowledging when a price is fair using "சரி" or returning to "கம்மி" (Less, from earlier episode).
17. **Roleplay - The Reluctant Buyer:** Full scene at a fruit cart mixing all Act 2 and 3 elements.

## Act 4: The Chaos Simulation - Mixed Drills (Minutes 15-20)
18. **Concept - Brain Switching:** Explaining that real life doesn't happen in categories. You take an auto, THEN you buy things.
19. **Drill - The Auto to the Market (Part 1):** Giving directions. Go straight, turn right, turn left.
20. **Drill - The Auto to the Market (Part 2):** Stop here. (Niruthunga). How much? (Evvalavu?) 
21. **Drill - The Auto to the Market (Part 3):** Driver says high price. Guest complains (Jaasthi).
22. **Story - The Victory:** Guest succeeds in the mental roleplay. Validating the progress built over the last 5 episodes.
23. **Drill - Zinger Callback:** Refreshing the previous episode's zinger: "உள்ள" (Inside) and relating it to getting inside the auto.
24. **Review - The Core Six:** Final rapid-fire validation of the 6 target words: வலது, இடது, எவ்வளவு, ஜாஸ்தி, ஒன்னு, பத்து.
25. **Outro:** Congratulating the listener. Delivering the new threshold Zinger ("Jaasthi!"). Readying them for the next episode.
