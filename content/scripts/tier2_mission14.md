# Tier 2 Mission 14: The Grocery Run

**Host:** வணக்கம், Agent. 
**Host:** We are slowing things down today. A long episode. A steady rhythm. 
**Host:** If you are walking to the store, or just wandering the neighborhood, keep your pace even. This isn't a sprint. It's a supply run. 
**Host:** You've mastered your anchor in the past tense. I said. 
**Guest:** சொன்னேன்.
**Host:** You own that now. That `-en` ending belongs to you. 
**Host:** But today, we move away from speaking, and focus on the hands. Holding, moving, and transacting. The mechanics of the market. 
**Host:** By the time you get home, you should be able to command the physical space around you.

## The Action Engine: Take & Put

**Host:** In the market, everything starts with a choice. You see something on the shelf. You want it. So you take it. 
**Host:** The command to take or pick something up is short. Sharp. "Take." 
**Guest:** எடு.
**Host:** Try it again. It literally means "pick it up". 
**Guest:** எடு.
**Host:** You see the milk. Take it. 
**Guest:** எடு.
**Host:** But wait, you look at the expiration date. You don't want it. You need to put it back. 
**Host:** Keep. Or put down. 
**Guest:** வை.
**Host:** Like "vai". Keep it there. 
**Guest:** வை.
**Host:** We have the fundamental binary of shopping. Take. 
**Guest:** எடு.
**Host:** Put. 
**Guest:** வை.
**Host:** I swear, husbands at the grocery store just see a snack aisle and immediately switch to "take" mode. They just look at chips and go "எடு". 
**Host:** Let's practice that toggle. Your hands moving through the aisles. 
**Host:** Take. 
**Guest:** எடு.
**Host:** Put down. 
**Guest:** வை.

[Pause: 4 seconds]

## The Action Engine: Bring & Give

**Host:** Okay, you've taken the items. Now you need to move them to the counter. Or your wife asks you to bring something from the other room. 
**Host:** "Bring." Or literally, "take and come".
**Guest:** கொண்டு வா.
**Host:** Notice how it ends in "vaa"—which you know means "come". You are commanding the object to come with you. Bring. 
**Guest:** கொண்டு வா.
**Host:** Bring the water. 
**Guest:** தண்ணி கொண்டு வா.
**Host:** Now you are at the counter. The items are in your hands. You need to hand them over. 
**Host:** "Give." 
**Guest:** குடு.
**Host:** Very quick sound. Give. 
**Guest:** குடு.
**Host:** Let's chain the whole physical sequence together. You are managing the inventory. 
**Host:** Take it. 
**Guest:** எடு.
**Host:** Bring it. 
**Guest:** கொண்டு வா.
**Host:** Give it. 
**Guest:** குடு.
**Host:** And if you change your mind? Put it down. 
**Guest:** வை.

## The Collision: Say & Ask

**Host:** Time to add the communication layer. You have the items, but you need to talk to the vendor. Earlier today, you were struggling slightly with the present and future tenses of "say" and "ask". Let's weave them into the grocery run. 
**Host:** You are pointing at the good tomatoes. You want Anna to take them. 
**Host:** "I am saying to take." Tell him what you are doing right now. 
**Guest:** எடு சொல்றேன்.
**Host:** Exactly. I am saying. சொல்றேன். 
**Host:** You go to the counter. The vendor rings up the bill. You don't know why it's so high. You asked him about it earlier. Look backward in time. 
**Host:** "I asked." 
**Guest:** கேட்டேன்.
**Host:** Right, that `-en` ending. The past is fixed. I asked. கேட்டேன்.
**Host:** He didn't answer. So you are probing again. Right now. In the present. 
**Host:** "I am asking you to give the bill." 
**Guest:** பில் குடு கேக்குறேன்.
**Host:** I am asking. கேக்குறேன். 
**Host:** Let the collision settle in. You have the hands and you have the mouth working together. 
**Host:** I am saying, take. 
**Guest:** எடு சொல்றேன். 
**Host:** I am asking you to give. 
**Guest:** குடு கேக்குறேன். 

[Pause: 4 seconds]

## The Kongu Respect Layer

**Host:** One vital detail before you leave the shop. In Coimbatore, shouting base verbs at merchants is considered rude. Unless they are much younger than you, we never just say "Take" or "Give". We add the magic Kongu layer of respect. 
**Host:** We add the "nga". 
**Host:** Polite take. Let's assume you're addressing an older merchant. 
**Guest:** எடுங்க.
**Host:** Polite give. 
**Guest:** குடுங்க.
**Host:** Polite put down. 
**Guest:** வைங்க.
**Host:** Try it with your respected uncle. "Mama, give the bag." 
**Guest:** மாமா, பை குடுங்க. 
**Host:** Beautiful. Keep those "nga"s loaded in your chamber for anyone older than you. 

[Pause: 4 seconds]

## The Horizon & Zinger

**Host:** Finally, imagine you are looking at something expensive. A giant silver tumbler. You don't want it right now. But maybe tomorrow. 
**Host:** Look to the horizon. "I will ask tomorrow." 
**Guest:** நாளைக்கு கேட்பேன்.
**Host:** Good. The `-peen` reaches into the future. 
**Host:** Now, for your Zinger. The merchant is being pushy. He wants you to buy the tumbler today. You want to deflect politely and get out of the store. You need to tell him you will buy it later. 
**Host:** Later, I will buy. "Naan appuram vaanguven". 
**Guest:** நான் அப்புறம் வாங்குவேன்.
**Host:** Let's hear the `-veen` ending for the future tense of buy. It pushes the purchase into the horizon. 
**Guest:** நான் அப்புறம் வாங்குவேன்.
**Host:** One more time, say it to the merchant with a polite nod as you back away. 
**Guest:** நான் அப்புறம் வாங்குவேன்.
**Host:** Excellent survival skills. You've navigated the market. You've handled the goods. The transaction is complete. Enjoy the rest of your walk, Agent. 
**Host:** சரி, போயிட்டு வரேன்.
