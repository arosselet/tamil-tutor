# Tier 2 Mission 9: The Pronoun Toggles (The Gossip Protocol)

## Word Status Sheet
- **TEACH:** அவங்க, அவன், அவ, அது, நம்ம, நாங்க
- **USE:** ஆமா, இல்ல, சரி, சாப்பிட்டேன், பசி, நேரம் ஆச்சு, ஏன்னா, அதனால, ஆனா, அண்ணா, அக்கா, இங்க, அங்க, எங்க, இருக்கு, நல்லா இருக்கு
- **CALLBACK:** தம்பி, தங்கச்சி, மாமா, அத்தை, முன்னாடி, இன்னைக்கு, பார்த்தேன்

## 1. The Intel Briefing

**Host:** Vanakkam, Agent. Today we enter the living room. Medium Energy. A narrative deep dive into what we call: The Gossip Protocol.
**Host:** You’re sitting at a family function in Coimbatore. People are moving around, whispers are being exchanged, and you need to track who did what. But in Tamil, you can't just point and say "He" or "She." 
**Host:** Our pronouns have a built-in respect toggle. If you use the casual form for an elder, it’s not a grammar mistake. It’s an insult. Today, we install your pronoun toggles.
[Pause: 2 seconds]

## 2. The Respect Toggle (Avanga)

**Host:** Let's start with the safest word in the Tamil language. If you are talking about an elder, a stranger, or anyone you respect, you use the respectful pronoun.
**Guest:** அவங்க.
**Host:** அவங்க. It technically means "They," but we use it for a single person to show respect. 
**Host:** Think of your older sister. Do you remember the word?
[Pause: 1 second]
**Guest:** அக்கா.
**Host:** சரி. If someone asks where she is, you point and say the respectful pronoun.
**Guest:** அவங்க.
**Host:** Your father-in-law? Remember the callback word for maternal uncle?
[Pause: 1 second]
**Guest:** மாமா.
**Host:** He gets the respect toggle too.
**Guest:** அவங்க.
[Pause: 2 seconds]

## 3. The Casual Toggle (Avan / Ava)

**Host:** Now, what if you are talking about someone younger? Like your younger brother. Remember that one?
[Pause: 1 second]
**Guest:** தம்பி.
**Host:** Right. For a younger male, you drop the respect toggle. You use the casual "He".
**Guest:** அவன்.
**Host:** அவன். And for a younger female? Like your younger sister?
[Pause: 1 second]
**Guest:** தங்கச்சி.
**Host:** You use the casual "She".
**Guest:** அவ.
**Host:** அவ.
**Host:** Let’s drill the radar. I name the relative. You give me the correct pronoun. The respect toggle must be instant.
**Host:** அண்ணா.
**Guest:** அவங்க.
**Host:** தங்கச்சி.
**Guest:** அவ.
**Host:** மாமா.
**Guest:** அவங்க.
**Host:** தம்பி.
**Guest:** அவன்.
**Host:** செம்ம. You have the basic toggles installed.
[Pause: 2 seconds]

## 4. The Trapdoor: Inclusive vs. Exclusive We

**Host:** Now for the most dangerous pronoun in Tamil. The word for "We". English only has one "We". Tamil has two. If you pick the wrong one, you accidentally insult people.
**Host:** Imagine you are talking to your mother-in-law, your அத்தை. You want to say, "We ate." But you mean just you and your wife. Not her. You use the exclusive "We".
**Guest:** நாங்க.
**Host:** நாங்க. It pushes the listener out of the group. 
**Host:** But what if you mean "All of us here, including you"? You use the inclusive "We".
**Guest:** நம்ம.
**Host:** நம்ம. It pulls the listener in. 
**Host:** Let’s test it. You are leaving the house. You tell your அத்தை that you and your wife are going out. Which "We" do you use?
[Pause: 1 second]
**Guest:** நாங்க.
**Host:** ஆமா! Because she is staying home. 
**Host:** Now, you are planning a family trip. You want to say "We are going," and you want her to come too. 
[Pause: 1 second]
**Guest:** நம்ம.
**Host:** Exactly. நம்ம is family. நாங்க is just us.
[Pause: 2 seconds]

## 5. The Non-Human Object (Adhu)

**Host:** The last pronoun is easy. If it’s an animal, an object, or a concept, it gets one word. "It" or "That".
**Guest:** அது.
**Host:** அது. You know the word for "is there."
**Guest:** அங்க இருக்கு.
**Host:** So if you see a good car, you point and say "It is there."
**Guest:** அது அங்க இருக்கு.
**Host:** And if someone asks about the food? You say "It is good."
**Guest:** அது நல்லா இருக்கு.
[Pause: 2 seconds]

## 6. The Gossip Simulation

**Host:** Alright, Agent. Let’s run the scenario. You are sitting with your அண்ணா. He missed the morning events and wants an update.
**Host:** He asks about தம்பி. You want to say: "I saw him today." Use your callback words.
[Pause: 2 seconds]
**Guest:** இன்னைக்கு அவன் பார்த்தேன்.
**Host:** Technically "அவனை பார்த்தேன்," but we focus on the root. அவன். Good.
**Host:** Now he asks about மாமா. You want to say: "I saw him before." Respect toggle on!
[Pause: 2 seconds]
**Guest:** முன்னாடி அவங்க பார்த்தேன்.

**Host:** Finally, he asks if you and the cousins are coming to the movie. You want to say "We are coming," but excluding him because he has to work.
[Pause: 1 second]
**Guest:** நாங்க.
[Pause: 2 seconds]
