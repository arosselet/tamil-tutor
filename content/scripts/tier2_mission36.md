## Part 2: The Breakdown

**Narrator (M):** One auto, two passengers who had never met, and a driver who had definitely met their type before. Let's look at the five words that kept the ride moving — more or less.

First: **ஏறு** (*Aeru*). To get in. To board. Murugan said it before anyone moved. Senthil said it to Meena. Meena said it back. Nobody went anywhere for a full minute. **ஏறு**.

Second: **இறங்கு** (*Irangu*). The other end. To get off. Meena announced it mid-exit. Murugan confirmed it. If **ஏறு** is the beginning of the ride, **இறங்கு** is the end. **இறங்கு**.

Third: **ஓடு** (*Oodu*). To run. But when Senthil said **ஓடு**, he meant *go* — move, drive. For a vehicle in motion, **ஓடு** is the word. The auto that went the wrong way? That also **ஓடு**-ed. Direction not included. **ஓடு**.

Fourth: **திரும்பு** (*Thirumbu*). To turn. Left or right was the argument. The word itself was never the problem. **திரும்பு**.

Fifth: **கவனி** (*Kavani*). Pay attention. Watch out. Be careful. Meena used it to warn Murugan about the one-way. Senthil used it for the speed bump. Murugan said he would **கவனி**. Then the speed bump happened. **கவனி**.

So: **ஏறு** to get in, **இறங்கு** to get out, **ஓடு** to go, **திரும்பு** to turn, and **கவனி** to watch out.

The auto made it. See you next mission.
