# Tier 2 Mission 11: Reported Speech (The Gossip Chain)

## Word Status Sheet

- **TEACH:** சொன்னாங்க (they said), கேட்டாங்க (they asked), சொன்னேன் (I said), கேட்டேன் (I asked), நினைச்சேன் (I thought), அப்படீங்களா? (is that so?), நிஜமா? (really?), புரிஞ்சுது (I understood), புரியல (I don't understand)
- **USE:** வணக்கம், சரி, ஆமா, இல்ல, இருக்கு, நல்லா இருக்கு, ஏன்னா, அதனால, ஆனா, அண்ணா, அக்கா, நீங்க, எனக்கு, உனக்கு, அவங்க, அவன், நாங்க, நம்ம, தெரியும், தெரியாது, சூப்பர், செம்ம, சொல்லுங்க, நேத்து, இப்போ, அப்புறம், போறேன், வரேன், சாப்பிட்டேன், சாப்டீங்களா?, பரவாயில்லை, என்ன?
- **CALLBACK:** தம்பி, தங்கச்சி, மாமா, அத்தை, பார்த்தேன், இன்னைக்கு, முன்னாடி

---

## 1. The Intel Briefing

**Host:** வணக்கம், Agent. Medium energy. We're going narrative today. Get your feet moving, this one's built for a walk.
**Host:** Here's the situation. You've been in Coimbatore for weeks now. You can order food, navigate an auto, identify every member of the family tree. செம்ம.
**Host:** ஆனா, there's one thing you still can't do. You can't report what someone else told you. And in a Tamil family, that's basically your full-time job.
[Pause: 2 seconds]
**Host:** Today we install your gossip engine. The ability to say: "He said this. She asked that. I thought something else." These are the verbs that turn you from a listener into a narrator.
[Pause: 2 seconds]

## 2. They Said (Sonnanga)

**Host:** Let's start with the most useful phrase in any family gathering. "They said." Remember, we use the respectful pronoun for elders.
**Guest:** அவங்க.
**Host:** Now stick the verb on it. "They said."
**Guest:** அவங்க சொன்னாங்க.
**Host:** சொன்னாங்க. That's the past tense of "to say" with the respectful plural ending. Say it again.
**Guest:** சொன்னாங்க.
[Pause: 1 second]
**Host:** Here's the scene. நேத்து, your அக்கா called. இப்போ your wife asks what she said. You say:
**Guest:** அக்கா சொன்னாங்க.
**Host:** ஆமா. But what did she say? "She said she's coming tomorrow."
**Guest:** அக்கா சொன்னாங்க, நாளைக்கு வரேன்.
**Host:** That's a full gossip report in one sentence. அக்கா சொன்னாங்க, நாளைக்கு வரேன். You just narrated someone else's words. That's the move.
[Pause: 2 seconds]

## 3. They Asked (Kettanga)

**Host:** Now the other side. Someone didn't tell you something, they asked you something. "They asked."
**Guest:** கேட்டாங்க.
**Host:** கேட்டாங்க. Same respectful ending. Let's use a callback word. Your uncle called. Who?
[Pause: 2 seconds]
**Guest:** மாமா.
**Host:** Your மாமா called. He asked, "Did you eat?" — the eternal question. You report this to your wife:
**Guest:** மாமா கேட்டாங்க, சாப்டீங்களா?
**Host:** செம்ம. மாமா கேட்டாங்க, சாப்டீங்களா? That's Tamil family life in one sentence. Someone always asks, and someone else always reports it.
[Pause: 2 seconds]

## 4. I Said / I Asked (Sonnen / Ketten)

**Host:** Now flip it. You're the one talking. "I said."
**Guest:** சொன்னேன்.
**Host:** சொன்னேன். And "I asked."
**Guest:** கேட்டேன்.
**Host:** கேட்டேன். 
**Host:** Scene. Your wife asks what happened at the shop. You say: "I asked how much." Then: "He said hundred." Use the callback word.
[Pause: 2 seconds]
**Guest:** கேட்டேன், எவ்ளோ? அவங்க சொன்னாங்க, நூறு.
**Host:** There we go. You're narrating a whole transaction. கேட்டேன் and சொன்னாங்க are your reporting pair. You ask, they answer. You report to your wife. That's the loop.
[Pause: 2 seconds]

## 5. I Thought (Ninaichchen)

**Host:** Now, the one that makes you sound like you've been here for years. "I thought."
**Guest:** நினைச்சேன்.
**Host:** நினைச்சேன். This word is gold. It softens everything. Instead of saying "He's wrong," you say "I thought it was different." Very Kongu.
**Host:** Your தங்கச்சி tells you the function is on Saturday. But you thought it was Sunday.
[Pause: 2 seconds]
**Guest:** நினைச்சேன், Sunday-ன்னு.
**Host:** ஆமா! நினைச்சேன், Sunday-ன்னு. "I thought it was Sunday." 
**Host:** The ன்னு at the end is a natural Kongu connector. It means "that" — as in "I thought THAT it was Sunday." You'll hear it everywhere.
[Pause: 2 seconds]

## 6. The Reactions (Appadingala? / Nijama?)

**Host:** Now, when someone reports gossip to you, you need reactions. You can't just sit there nodding.
**Host:** Reaction one. Mild surprise, polite interest. "Is that so?"
**Guest:** அப்படீங்களா?
**Host:** அப்படீங்களா? This is your safest all-purpose reaction. Works in every situation.
**Host:** Reaction two. Genuine shock. "Really?"
**Guest:** நிஜமா?
**Host:** நிஜமா? Shorter, punchier. Use this when the gossip is actually interesting.
**Host:** Let's drill them. அக்கா சொன்னாங்க, மாமா புது house வாங்கினாங்க.
[Pause: 1 second]
**Guest:** அப்படீங்களா?
**Host:** அவங்க சொன்னாங்க, it cost fifty lakhs.
**Guest:** நிஜமா?!
**Host:** Perfect instinct. Mild for the setup, explosive for the punchline. That's how gossip works.
[Pause: 2 seconds]

## 7. Understanding and Not Understanding

**Host:** Two more tools for the gossip chain. When someone explains something and it clicks:
**Guest:** புரிஞ்சுது.
**Host:** புரிஞ்சுது. "I understood." And when it doesn't click at all:
**Guest:** புரியல.
**Host:** புரியல. "I don't understand."
**Host:** These are your honest signals. Your அத்தை is explaining something fast in Tamil. You catch the gist:
[Pause: 2 seconds]
**Guest:** புரிஞ்சுது.
**Host:** ஆனா then she switches to something complicated and you're lost:
**Guest:** புரியல.
**Host:** பரவாயில்லை. That's honest. புரியல is never embarrassing. It's a request for patience.
[Pause: 2 seconds]

## 8. The Full Gossip Simulation

**Host:** சரி, Agent. Time to run the full chain. You are sitting in the living room. Your அண்ணா just got off the phone. He looks at you.
**Host:** He says: "மாமா called."
**Host:** What do you say?
[Pause: 2 seconds]
**Guest:** என்ன சொன்னாங்க?
**Host:** "What did they say?" Perfect question. என்ன சொன்னாங்க.
**Host:** அண்ணா says: "He asked if நாங்க are coming for the function."
**Host:** Your reaction?
[Pause: 2 seconds]
**Guest:** அப்படீங்களா?
**Host:** He says: "I said ஆமா, நாங்க வரேன்."
**Host:** But you didn't know about this. You thought it was next week.
[Pause: 2 seconds]
**Guest:** நினைச்சேன், next week-ன்னு.
**Host:** He laughs. He says it's this weekend. You don't fully get the details.
**Guest:** புரியல. எப்போ?
**Host:** He says Saturday morning.
**Guest:** புரிஞ்சுது. சரி.
**Host:** And then your wife walks in and asks what happened. You narrate: "மாமா கேட்டாங்க, function-க்கு வரீங்களா-ன்னு. அண்ணா சொன்னாங்க, ஆமா."
[Pause: 2 seconds]
**Host:** That's a three-person gossip chain. You just ran the full loop. You received, reacted, and reported.
[Pause: 3 seconds]

**Guest:** அப்படீங்களா?
