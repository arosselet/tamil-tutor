# Breakdown: Tier 2, Mission 22 — The Preference Protocol

**HOST:** Welcome back, Andrew. You just survived the Coimbatore dining table minefield. Navigating Mother-in-law's cooking is one thing, but having a movie preference? That's the real test of citizenship.

**HOST:** Before we break it down, let's go back to that moment when the bitter gourd hit the plate. Did you hear how Andrew handled it?

**[REPLAY INTERCEPT CLIP: Andrew saying "Deepa, எனக்கு Pavakkai... பிடிக்காது."]**

**HOST:** "எனக்கு Pavakkai... பிடிக்காது." (Enakku Pavakkai... pidikkaadhu).

**HOST:** Today we’re mastering the two words that give you a personality in Tamil: `பிடிக்கும்` (Pidikkum) and `பிடிக்காது` (Pidikkaadhu).

**HOST:** In English, we say "I like it." But in Tamil, the structure is different. It’s more like "To me, it is pleasing." That "To me" part is **எனக்கு** (Enakku). We've used it for hunger (`எனக்கு பசி`) and thirst (`எனக்கு தாகம்`). Now we use it for taste.

**HOST:** `எனக்கு பிடிக்கும்` — I like. `எனக்கு பிடிக்காது` — I don't like.

**HOST:** Say it with me. **பிடிக்கும்**. (Pidikkum). One more time. **பிடிக்கும்**.

**HOST:** And the negative: **பிடிக்காது**. (Pidikkaadhu). It ends with that long "aa" sound. **பிடிக்காது**.

**HOST:** Let's practice. I'll give you a noun, you say you like it. Coffee? 
**Andrew (Prompt):** எனக்கு Coffee பிடிக்கும்.

**HOST:** How about Tea? 
**Andrew (Prompt):** எனக்கு Tea பிடிக்கும்.

**HOST:** Now, the bitter gourd. Pavakkai. Say you don't like it.
**Andrew (Prompt):** எனக்கு Pavakkai பிடிக்காது.

**HOST:** Perfect. Now, there was another word Amma used when the Rajini trailer came on. She asked: **"உனக்கு வேற எந்த actors பிடிக்கும்?"** (What other actors do you like?).

**HOST:** And Deepa ended the scene with a very useful word: **இஷ்டம்** (Ishttam).

**HOST:** `இஷ்டம்` means preference, liking, or choice. When Deepa said **"உன் இஷ்டம்"** (Un ishttam), she was saying "It's your choice" or "Whatever you prefer."

**HOST:** It’s a great way to dodge a decision. If someone asks what you want for dinner, you can say **"உன் இஷ்டம்"** to your wife. It means "Whatever you like."

**HOST:** Say it: **இஷ்டம்**. (Ishttam). Like a choice or a wish.

**HOST:** Let's look at the "Rajini Factor" in that scene. In Tamil Nadu, especially in a house in Coimbatore, Rajini isn't just an actor; he’s an event. When Amma asked **"உனக்கு Rajini படம் பிடிக்குமா?"**, she wasn't just asking about movies. She was asking if you’re on the team.

**HOST:** Andrew’s answer was solid: **"எனக்கு Rajini ரொம்ப பிடிக்கும்."** (I really like Rajini).

**HOST:** Remember, `ரொம்ப` (Romba) means "a lot" or "very." You can use it with anything you like. **எனக்கு Biryani ரொம்ப பிடிக்கும்.** **எனக்கு Coimbatore ரொம்ப பிடிக்கும்.**

**HOST:** One final callback to an old word. Remember **பார்த்தேன்** (I saw)? Andrew used it to talk about the trailer. **" trailer பார்த்தேன்."**

**HOST:** Today's mission was about the **"எனக்கு"** (To me) pattern. Once you master this, you can stick any English noun in there. "எனக்கு Netflix பிடிக்கும்." "எனக்கு Gym பிடிக்காது."

**HOST:** Go back and listen to the Intercept again. This time, listen for that **எனக்கு... பிடிக்கும்** pattern. It’s everywhere.

**HOST:** I'll see you in the field. **சரி, போயிட்டு வரேன்!**
