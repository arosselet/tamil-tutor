# Mission 25: The Dative Emotion Pattern

**Thambi (M):** மாமா, **சாப்பிடுங்க**. Why are you just staring at the sambar? **என்னாச்சு?**

**Mama (M):** One **நிமிஷம்** let me think. It's about this **கல்யாணம்**. The budget is going crazy. Too many relatives, too many functions.

**Thambi (M):** Oh, the big family wedding. It’s a huge party, மாமா! Why are you so serious?

**Mama (M):** It's not just a party. I have to manage everything. **நிஜமா**, it's too much pressure. **எனக்கு கவலையா இருக்கு**. 

**Akka (F):** (Walking in) Hey! Everyone is talking about the groom's family. They are so good. I just saw their photos. **எனக்கு சந்தோஷம்**! I’m so happy for the bride.

**Mama (M):** **சந்தோஷம்** for you, but **கவலை** for me! I’m the one signing the cheques. This hall they chose... **முன்னாடி** we checked that other place, and it was cheaper. This is a big **தப்பு**. 

**Akka (F):** மாமா, **கோவப்படாதீங்க**. Don't be angry. It’s a once-in-a-lifetime event. 

**Thambi (M):** Exactly. Although, Akka, did you hear the latest rumour about that wedding hall? 

**Akka (F):** Rumour? What rumour?

**Thambi (M):** They say the old owner’s ghost still wanders the kitchen at night. 

**Akka (F):** Ghost? **நிஜமா?** Oh my god, **எனக்கு பயமா இருக்கு**! Thambi, stop it! 

**Mama (M):** **சும்மா இருங்க**. Both of you. Thambi, that's **தப்பு**. Don't scare your sister. Now, **வை**. Keep that plate on the table and let's finish lunch. 

**Thambi (M):** **சரி**, மாமா. But I’m still excited for the food at the wedding. **எனக்கு** biryani **பிடிக்கும்**!

**Akka (F):** **எனக்கு** that sweet halwa **பிடிக்கும்**. See? We are all happy again. 

**Mama (M):** **சரி சரி**. Let’s eat. 

[Pause: 3 sec]

**Analyst Maya (F):** Welcome back to the debrief. Did you catch the shift in that dining room? It went from wedding stress to ghost stories in about thirty seconds.

**Analyst Raj (M):** Typical family lunch. But Maya, did you hear how they were talking about their feelings? It wasn't just "I am happy" or "I am worried."

**Analyst Maya (F):** Exactly. That’s our mission for today. The **Dative Emotion Pattern**. In Tamil, emotions don't happen *to* you as a state of being—they are things that "exist for you."

**Analyst Raj (M):** Right. It all starts with that one word: **எனக்கு**. To me.

**Analyst Maya (F):** Let's go back to when Akka walked in. She was so excited about the wedding news. She didn't say "I am happy." She said:

**Akka (F):** "**எனக்கு சந்தோஷம்**!"

**Analyst Raj (M):** **எனக்கு சந்தோஷம்**. "To me, joy." It sounds so much more grounded than the English version. 

**Analyst Maya (F):** It really is. **எனக்கு** is the anchor. You remember **எனக்கு**, right Raj? We used it for food earlier. **எனக்கு வேணும்**. "To me, it is wanted."

**Analyst Raj (M):** Yeah, I remember. But then Mama used it for something much heavier. He was looking at the wedding bills and he said:

**Mama (M):** "**எனக்கு கவலையா இருக்கு**."

**Analyst Maya (F):** **கவலை** is worry. But listen to how he ends it: **எனக்கு கவலையா இருக்கு**. It literally means "To me, it is worrisome."

**Analyst Raj (M):** So why the **இருக்கு** at the end of that one, but not for **சந்தோஷம்**?

**Analyst Maya (F):** Great catch. **சந்தோஷம்** (Joy) is like a noun you possess. **கவலை** (Worry) and **பயம்** (Fear) often take that **ஆ இருக்கு** ending to show it’s an active state. Like, "it is being worrisome to me."

**Analyst Raj (M):** Ah, okay. So when Thambi started telling that ghost story and Akka got freaked out, she said:

**Akka (F):** "**எனக்கு பயமா இருக்கு**!"

**Analyst Maya (F):** Exactly. **எனக்கு பயமா இருக்கு**. "To me, it is fearful." She’s not just "scared"—she’s experiencing the state of fear. 

**Analyst Raj (M):** I love that. It’s like the emotion is a visitor in the room.

**Analyst Maya (F):** It is! And notice how this same pattern—this **எனக்கு** anchor—is what we use for likes and dislikes too. Remember the end of the scene?

**Thambi (M):** "**எனக்கு** biryani **பிடிக்கும்**!"

**Akka (F):** "**எனக்கு** that sweet halwa **பிடிக்கும்**."

**Analyst Raj (M):** **பிடிக்கும்**. "It pleases me." Or "To me, it is liked." It’s all the same logic. **எனக்கு** plus the thing you're feeling.

**Analyst Maya (F):** You’ve got it. So, if you’re happy with this breakdown, what would you say?

**Analyst Raj (M):** **எனக்கு சந்தோஷம்**! 

**Analyst Maya (F):** **செம்ம**! And if you're worried about the next mission?

**Analyst Raj (M):** **எனக்கு கவலையா இருக்கு**... but only a little bit.

**Analyst Maya (F):** (Laughs) Don't worry. You're doing great. Let's do one more replay. Listen to Mama one last time. He's stressed, he's paying for everything, and he says:

**Mama (M):** "**எனக்கு கவலையா இருக்கு**."

**Analyst Raj (M):** **கவலையா இருக்கு**. Got it. 

**Analyst Maya (F):** **சரி**, that's a wrap for the dining room drama. See you at the next location.

**Analyst Raj (M):** **போயிட்டு வரேன்**!
