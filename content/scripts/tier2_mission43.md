# Mission 43 — The Confirmation Window

## Intercept

**Host A (F):** (Frantic tapping sounds) This is impossible. Anna picking up the phone is like waiting for rain in July. Phone-அ தூக்கு (thūkku) அண்ணா! தூக்குங்க!

**Host B (M):** (Laughing) Relax-ஆ இருங்க. Why are you so stressed?

**Host A (F):** 5 minutes-ல booking cancel ஆயிடும். I'm calling (kūppiḍu) him for the tenth time. அண்ணா-வ கூப்பிடுறேன் (kūppiḍuṟēn), but he’s not picking up!

**Host B (M):** சரி சரி, Phone-அ இங்க வை (vai). Just keep the phone down. Message அனுப்பு (anuppu). He’ll see that faster.

**Host A (F):** ஏற்கனவே அனுப்புனேன் (anuppunēn). I sent it already! But reply வரல. அவருக்கு இன்னைக்கு நேரம் இருக்கா (nēram irukkā)? Does he even have time today?

**Host B (M):** Group chat-ல மத்தவங்ககிட்ட கேளுங்க.

**Host A (F):** கேட்டுட்டு தான் இருக்கேன். I am asking (kēṭkuṟēn) everyone. யாரும் பதில் சொல்லல. (Phone pings) Oh! கிடைச்சுது (kiḍaiccuđu)! Message கிடைச்சுது!

**Host B (M):** என்னாச்சு? Did he confirm?

**Host A (F):** (Sighs) No. He just sent: "சாப்டீங்களா (sāpṭīngalā)?"

**Host B (M):** (Bursts out laughing) Classic! Standard Coimbatore protocol. First, confirm the stomach, then confirm the plan.

**Host A (F):** No time for this! Tell him to கன்ஃபர்ம் (kanphārm) the lunch booking right now!

## Breakdown

**Analyst Maya (F):** (Laughs) I feel for her, Raj. That "Confirmation Window" stress is very real. But of course, Anna only wants to know if they’ve eaten.

**Analyst Raj (M):** It’s the ultimate deflection, Maya! You can’t be mad at someone asking if you’ve eaten. சாப்டீங்களா (sāpṭīngalā)? It’s like a social reset button.

**Analyst Maya (F):** Exactly. But before they got to the food, we had some really useful "action" words. Like when she was yelling at the phone: தூக்கு (thūkku).

**Analyst Raj (M):** Yeah, தூக்கு (thūkku) literally means "lift" or "pick up." So, "Phone-அ தூக்கு" is "Pick up the phone." And then the opposite, which you told her to do: வை (vai).

**Analyst Maya (F):** Right, வை (vai) is "keep" or "put." "Phone-அ இங்க வை" — keep the phone here. We’ve seen Andrew struggle with these two, so seeing them as a "Pick up / Put down" pair is perfect.

**Analyst Raj (M):** And then the communication verbs. She said "I am calling him" — அண்ணா-வ கூப்பிடுறேன் (kūppiḍuṟēn).

**Analyst Maya (F):** கூப்பிடு (kūppiḍu) is the root for "call." Whether you're calling someone across the street or on the phone. And for texting, we use அனுப்பு (anuppu).

**Analyst Raj (M):** To send. "Message அனுப்பு" — send a message. And the best part? When she finally got that reply. கிடைச்சுது (kiḍaiccuđu).

**Analyst Maya (F):** I love that word. கிடைச்சுது (kiḍaiccuđu). It means "I got it" or "it was received." It’s so satisfying to say when that notification finally pings.

**Analyst Raj (M):** It really is. But then she gets hit with the availability question. நேரம் இருக்கா (nēram irukkā)? "Do you have time?"

**Analyst Maya (F):** நேரம் (nēram) is time. இருக்கா (irukkā) is "is there?" or "do you have?" So, "நேரம் இருக்கா" is the essential "Are you free?" check.

**Analyst Raj (M):** And finally, the goal of the whole frantic scene: கன்ஃபர்ம் (kanphārm). We just use the English word there, but with that nice Tamil "m" ending. கன்ஃபர்ம் பண்ணு. Confirm it.

**Analyst Maya (F):** (Laughs) Well, hopefully, Anna eventually stops asking about lunch and actually confirms the lunch.

**Analyst Raj (M):** Knowing Coimbatore families? They’ll still be asking "சாப்டீங்களா" while they’re sitting down to eat.
