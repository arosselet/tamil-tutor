## Part 2: The Breakdown

**Narrator (M):** Family festivals are 10% prayer and 90% figuring out who everyone is. Kavitha and Rajesh just used five essential words for navigating the family roster.

First: **யாரு** (*Yaaru*). Who. The foundation of all gossip. **யாரு** அது? Who is that? Simple, direct, and very necessary. **யாரு**.

Second and Third: **சொன்னான்** (*Sonnaan*) and **சொன்னா** (*Sonnaa*). He said and She said. These are the informal past tense forms of "to say." Anna **சொன்னான்** — He said. Akka **சொன்னா** — She said. In Coimbatore, these are the engines of every report. **சொன்னான்**. **சொன்னா**.

Fourth: **நினைச்சேன்** (*Ninaichhen*). I thought. Used when you're making a guess or realizing you were wrong. Rajesh **நினைச்சேன்** it was a surprise. Kavitha **நினைச்சேன்** it was a relative. **நினைச்சேன்**.

Fifth: **தெரியும்** (*Theriyum*). To know, or literally, "it is known." In the negative, Kavitha said "I don't **தெரியும்** her," which is perfect Thanglish for "I don't know her." It’s the word for familiarity and facts. **தெரியும்**.

So: **யாரு** for who, **சொன்னான்** and **சொன்னா** for he and she said, **நினைச்சேன்** for what you thought, and **தெரியும்** for what you know.
