# Script: Level 4, Episode 3 — Chronology Glue

**Host:** Welcome back to Level 4, The Debrief.
**Guest:** I am fully prepared to tell you everything I did in the past. 
**Host:** Yes, you learned the Core Five consumption verbs. I ate. நான் சாப்பிட்டேன். I slept. நான் தூங்கினேன். But sounding fluent isn't just about throwing verbs at people. It's about connecting them. First this, then that. Before, after, yesterday, today.
**Guest:** Storytelling glue!
**Host:** Exactly. We call it Chronology Glue. Today, you learn how to sequence your life. Let's start with the grand timeline. Yesterday and Today. 
**Guest:** The classics.
**Host:** The Tamil word for "Yesterday" is நேத்து.
**Guest:** நேத்து.
**Host:** Let's glue it to what you know. Yesterday, I slept.
**Guest:** நேத்து தூங்கினேன்.
**Host:** Yesterday, I watched a movie.
**Guest:** நேத்து movie பார்த்தேன்.
**Host:** And the word for "Today" is இன்னைக்கு.
**Guest:** இன்னைக்கு.
**Host:** Today, I woke up. 
**Guest:** இன்னைக்கு எழுந்தேன்.
**Host:** Now listen to that nasal ending. இன்னைக்கு. There's a slight bounce to it. Today, I drank coffee.
**Guest:** இன்னைக்கு coffee குடிச்சேன்.
**Host:** Okay, rapid fire time. Yesterday, I ate.
**Guest:** நேத்து சாப்பிட்டேன்.
**Host:** Today, I ate.
**Guest:** இன்னைக்கு சாப்பிட்டேன்.

---

**Host:** Act Two. Internal chronologies. When you are telling a story, what is the most important word to establish order?
**Guest:** Probably the word "First".
**Host:** Bingo. "First, I did this". The Tamil word for "First" is முதல்ல. 
**Guest:** முதல்ல.
**Host:** It's super useful for giving instructions to auto drivers too, but right now, let's use it for the debrief. Let's say your spouse asks about your morning. You start with: First, I woke up.
**Guest:** முதல்ல எழுந்தேன்.
**Host:** Perfect. First I drank coffee.
**Guest:** முதல்ல coffee குடிச்சேன்.
**Host:** And what comes after "First"? Usually "After that" or "Then". The Tamil phrase for "After that" is அதுக்கு அப்புறம்.
**Guest:** அதுக்கு அப்புறம்.
**Host:** Let's break it down. "Apuram" means after later. "Adhukku" means "to that". So, after that. அதுக்கு அப்புறம். This is the single most common glue word in spoken Tamil storytelling.
**Guest:** அதுக்கு அப்புறம்.
**Host:** Somebody asks what you did. "First, I ate dosa. After that, I slept." Let's build it. First, I ate dosa.
**Guest:** முதல்ல dosa சாப்பிட்டேன்.
**Host:** After that, I slept.
**Guest:** அதுக்கு அப்புறம் தூங்கினேன்.
**Host:** Beautiful! See how suddenly you sound like a native storyteller? You aren't just listing verbs; you have a flow. First I watched TV.
**Guest:** முதல்ல TV பார்த்தேன்.
**Host:** After that, I drank water. Water is thanni.
**Guest:** அதுக்கு அப்புறம் thanni குடிச்சேன்.

---

**Host:** Act Three. Reversing the clock. What if things were out of order? The word for "Before" or "In front of" or "Previously" is முன்னாடி.
**Guest:** முன்னாடி.
**Host:** In Level 3, you learned directional words like left and right. முன்னாடி can mean "in front of" spatially, but in a timeline, it means "Before". 
**Guest:** So it's like "Before I came here" or "Before that". 
**Host:** Exactly. If "After that" is அதுக்கு அப்புறம், you can use முன்னாடி just by itself to mean "Before". For example, "Before, I drank coffee."
**Guest:** முன்னாடி coffee குடிச்சேன்.
**Host:** Very good. Before, I ate. 
**Guest:** முன்னாடி சாப்பிட்டேன்.
**Host:** Let's mix up all the timeline words so far. Yesterday.
**Guest:** நேத்து.
**Host:** Today.
**Guest:** இன்னைக்கு.
**Host:** First.
**Guest:** முதல்ல.
**Host:** After that.
**Guest:** அதுக்கு அப்புறம்.
**Host:** Before.
**Guest:** முன்னாடி. 

---

**Host:** Act Four. The Master Sequencer. This is the big loop. I am going to challenge you to string these together without pausing. Are you ready?
**Guest:** I think so. Glue and verbs. Let's go.
**Host:** Scene: Monday morning at the office. Your colleague asks how your weekend was. You say: Yesterday, I watched TV. First, I ate dosa. After that, I drank coffee. Let's go line by line. Yesterday, I watched TV.
**Guest:** நேத்து TV பார்த்தேன்.
**Host:** First, I ate dosa.
**Guest:** முதல்ல dosa சாப்பிட்டேன்.
**Host:** After that, I drank coffee.
**Guest:** அதுக்கு அப்புறம் coffee குடிச்சேன்.
**Host:** Now another day. Today I woke up.
**Guest:** இன்னைக்கு எழுந்தேன்.
**Host:** After that I ate.
**Guest:** அதுக்கு அப்புறம் சாப்பிட்டேன்.
**Host:** Before, I slept.
**Guest:** முன்னாடி தூங்கினேன்.
**Host:** Okay, let's do a callback from Level 2. Food questions! Do you remember how to say "Is it spicy?" 
**Guest:** காரமா?
**Host:** Nice! Someone gives you food. You say: "First, I ate. After that, it is spicy." Careful: "is spicy" is the present tense describing state. It's just Kaaram. Just string them. First I ate.
**Guest:** முதல்ல சாப்பிட்டேன்.
**Host:** After that, it is spicy!
**Guest:** அதுக்கு அப்புறம் காரம். 
**Host:** It's disjointed English, but perfect colloquial Tamil. You are communicating reality. Let's review the five glue words one last time. Yesterday.
**Guest:** நேத்து.
**Host:** Today.
**Guest:** இன்னைக்கு.
**Host:** First.
**Guest:** முதல்ல.
**Host:** After that.
**Guest:** அதுக்கு அப்புறம்.
**Host:** Before.
**Guest:** முன்னாடி.
**Host:** Your Zinger for today. To say "First, listen to me". The word for "tell" or "say" is சொல்லுங்க. Listen carefully: I'm going to say it, you repeat it. "First, I tell [you]". Or practically, "Let me speak first". The phrase is: முதல்ல நான் சொல்றேன். Naan solren means I'm telling. Say it.
**Guest:** முதல்ல நான் சொல்றேன்.
**Host:** Excellent. Use that when you need to take control of the timeline! See you in the next episode.
