## Part 2: The Breakdown

**Narrator (M):** High tension in the kitchen. Ravi is learning that cooking with his mother-in-law is a sequence of precise commands. Let's look at the five verbs that kept the sambar on track.

First, the big goal: **சமை** (*Samai*). To cook. Ravi's mother-in-law said we have to **சமை** fast. **சமை** is the root for everything happening in the kitchen.

Next, the preparation: **கழுவு** (*Kazhuvu*). To wash. Not just for vegetables, but also at the end when she told Ravi to **கழுவு** his hands. **கழுவு**.

Then, the liquid: **ஊத்து** (*Oothu*). To pour. Whether it's water into the pot or oil into the pan, the instruction is always **ஊத்து**. **ஊத்து**.

Then, the action: **கலக்கு** (*Kalakku*). To mix or stir. Ravi used the spoon to **கலக்கு** the spices and the mustard seeds. **கலக்கு**.

Finally, the lid: **மூடு** (*Moodu*). To close or cover. She told him to **மூடு** the pot to keep the steam in. And remember, the opposite was **திற** — to open — but she told him *not* to do that yet. **மூடு** — to close.

So, the map: **சமை** to cook, **கழுவு** to wash, **ஊத்து** to pour, **கலக்கு** to mix, and **மூடு** to close.

The sambar survived. Ravi might too. See you next mission.
