# Tier 2, Mission 42 — The Change Problem

**Cast:**
- **Host A (F):** Urban, sharp, code-switches naturally. Playing the customer.
- **Host B (M):** Warm, local, tea stall energy. Playing the vendor.
- **Analyst Maya (F):** Pattern-focused, sharp.
- **Analyst Raj (M):** Story-focused, Coimbatore local flavor.

---

## Part 1: The Intercept

**[SFX: Tea stall ambience — cups clinking, chatter, boiling water.]**

**Host B (M):** (Brisk, but welcoming) வாங்க! என்ன வேணுமுங்க?

**Host A (F):** One tea. (Handing over money) இதோ, நூறு ரூபாய் **கொடுத்தேன்**.

**Host B (M):** (Returning change) இந்தாங்க, fifty change, madam.

**Host A (F):** (Sharp) Wait wait. நான் நூறு **கொடுத்தேன்**! Eighty change **எங்க**?

**Host B (M):** (Certain) இல்லீங்க madam, fifty தான் **கொடுத்தேன்**. Tea twenty, thirty back. See?

**Host A (F):** (Escalating) இல்ல! நான் **கேட்குறேன்** — **எங்க** எனக்கு proper change?

**Host B (M):** (Firm) நான் என்ன சொல்றேன்னா, fifty தான். Counter பாருங்க. 

**Host A (F):** (Frustrated) **புரியல**! How is fifty, fifty? நூறு தான் **கொடுத்தேன்** — **அதான்** சொல்றேன்!

**Host B (M):** (Genuinely apologetic) சரி சரிங்க madam. மன்னிச்சுக்கங்க. நூறு இங்கேயே இருக்கு. Counter கீழ போயிட்டு.

**Host A (F):** (Vindicated) **அதான்**! **அதான்** நான் சொன்னது! நான் நூறு தான் **கொடுத்தேன்**!

**Host B (M):** (Handing correct change) இதோ, eighty change. **வை**-யுங்க madam.

**Host A (F):** (Firm, taking her tea) இனிமேல் காசை சரியா **வை**-யுங்க. நான் இங்க **வந்தேன்** டீ குடிக்க — இப்படி சண்டை போட இல்ல!

**Host B (M):** (Nodding) சரிங்க madam. நாளைக்கு **கேட்பேன்** சரியா. இந்த problem வராது.

**Host A (F):** (Sipping, softer) **புரிஞ்சுது**.

## Part 2: The Breakdown

**Analyst Maya:** Raj, that vendor had a very bad afternoon.

**Analyst Raj:** (Laughing) Classic Coimbatore tea stall energy. The note slips under the counter tray, and suddenly both sides are completely certain they're right. 

**Analyst Maya:** The word that drove the whole fight — **கொடுத்தேன்** (*kodutten*). "I gave." Both of them kept firing it at each other. The customer: "I **கொடுத்தேன்** a hundred." The vendor: "No, you **கொடுத்தேன்** fifty." Same word, same certainty, opposite claims.

**Analyst Raj:** That's what makes **கொடுத்தேன்** such a loaded word. It's past tense, first person. Finished action. No ambiguity. The root is **கொடு** — to give — and the past first-person ending stacks onto it: **கொடுத்தேன்**. And once you say it, you sound completely certain.

**Analyst Maya:** Paired with it — **வந்தேன்** (*vanden*). "I came." She saved it for the end — I **வந்தேன்** for tea, not for a fight. 

**Analyst Raj:** **வந்தேன்** isn't just travel — it's the fact that you showed up. The root is **வா** (come), and the same past ending gives you **வந்தேன்**. Hear the pattern? Both **கொடுத்தேன்** and **வந்தேன்** end the same way. **ேன்** (*-en*). Past tense, first person.

**Analyst Maya:** Now the word I loved most — **அதான்** (*adaan*). When that note appeared from under the tray — **அதான்**!

**Analyst Raj:** (Laughing) **அதான்** is pure vindication. "That's why. Exactly. See, I told you." It's a connector with attitude. You drop it alone — **அதான்** — and the whole room knows you've been proven right.

**Analyst Maya:** Then the confusion arc. First she hit **புரியல** (*puriyala*) — "I don't understand." 

**Analyst Raj:** **புரியல** is the word for when logic has failed you. When something should make sense and doesn't. 

**Analyst Maya:** And then the close — **புரிஞ்சுது** (*purinjudhu*). "Got it. Understood." She didn't forgive him, she didn't apologize. Just: **புரிஞ்சுது**. Case closed. 

**Analyst Raj:** The callbacks landed well too. **கேட்குறேன்** — "I am asking" — came out mid-dispute. Not a polite question. A demand. And the vendor closed with **கேட்பேன்** — "I will ask." Future tense promise. Same root, different time. **கேட்குறேன்** is now, **கேட்பேன்** is will. 

**Analyst Maya:** And **வை** (*vai*) — we've been struggling with this one, but here it worked. "Keep it" or "Place it." The vendor says "**வை**-யுங்க" — keep the change. The customer says "proper-ஆ **வை**-யுங்க" — place it properly. 

**Analyst Raj:** And **எங்க?** (*enga*) — "where?" — was her opening shot. Short. Loaded. Just: **எங்க**? 

**Analyst Maya:** Let's replay the moment the note appeared. Listen for **அதான்**.

**Analyst Maya:** Always check under the tray.
