# Mission Script: Tier 2, Mission 47 — The Weekend Plan

**Host A (F):** [SFX: Phone ringing, answered] [SFX: Grunting, heavy thud] ஹலோ? என்ன பண்றீங்க? What are you doing? It sounds like you're fighting a bear.

**Host B (M):** [SFX: Heavy breathing] பியர்-ஆ? இல்ல... I'm just cleaning my room. It's a disaster. இப்போ இந்த பழைய பெட்டி எல்லாம் **தூக்கு**-றேன். I'm lifting all these old boxes.

**Host A (F):** [Laughing] பெட்டி-யா? Why now? I called because of the weekend plans. 

**Host B (M):** [SFX: Another thud] இருங்க... இதோ இங்க **வை**-க்கிறேன். [Pause: 1 sec] சரி, சொல்லுங்க. What plans?

**Host A (F):** Everyone wants to go for a movie on Saturday. நாளைக்கு **ஃப்ரீ-யா?** are you free tomorrow?

**Host B (M):** நாளைக்கு-வா? தெரியல. இப்போ எனக்கு **நேரம் இல்ல.** I have no time to think about tomorrow while I'm moving this stuff.

**Host A (F):** but I need to know! do you have time after you finish that? அப்புறம் **நேரம் இருக்கா?** 

**Host B (M):** இருக்கலாம். did you talk to Rahul?

**Host A (F):** [Pause: 1 sec] அவன்கிட்ட இப்போ **கேட்குறேன்.** I'm asking him right now. He's not picking up yet.

**Host B (M):** சரி, இருங்க. இதை முடிச்சிட்டு நானும் அவன்கிட்ட **கேட்பேன்.** I will ask him too. but for now, don't rush me.

**Host A (F):** யாரும் ஒன்னும் சொல்லலன்னா, I'm going to **கேன்சல்** the whole thing. forget it.

**Host B (M):** **கேன்சல்**-ஆ? **நிஜமா?** really? don't do that. I want to see that movie. 

**Host A (F):** then tell me! are you free or not?

**Host B (M):** பாருங்க, Saturday கஷ்டம். can we just **போஸ்ட்போன்** to Sunday? Sunday is better for everyone.

**Host A (F):** [Sighs] **போஸ்ட்போன்** to Sunday? சரி. I'll ask the group. ஆனா Sunday-வும் முடியலன்னா, it's definitely a **கேன்சல்**.

**Host B (M):** [SFX: Heavy lifting grunt] சரி, okay. I'll finish this and call you. இதோ இதையும் **தூக்கு**-றேன்... அப்புறம் இங்க **வை**-க்கிறேன். Bye! [SFX: Phone hangs up]

---

**Analyst Maya (F):** [Laughing] I feel for Host B. Trying to move heavy boxes while someone is grilling you about weekend movie plans is the worst.

**Analyst Raj (M):** It really is! But he handled it well. And we got some great scheduling "glue" in there. Maya, did you catch the first one? **ஃப்ரீ-யா?**

**Analyst Maya (F):** Oh, that’s a classic Coimbatore Thanglish special. Just "Free" plus the question marker **யா**. **ஃப்ரீ-யா?** are you free? It’s so much more common than asking something formal.

**Analyst Raj (M):** Exactly. And then when he said he was busy, she asked: **நேரம் இருக்கா?** nér-am iru-kkā? Do you have time?

**Analyst Maya (F):** **நேரம்**. time. and **இருக்கா?** is there? It’s literally "Is there time?" Very useful for any situation where you're checking someone's availability. 

**Analyst Raj (M):** But my favorite part was the verb contrast. This is where Andrew’s been hitting a wall lately. Host A said she was asking Rahul *right then*: **கேட்குறேன்**. két-ku-rén.

**Analyst Maya (F):** Right. That **கு** in the middle—**கேட்குறேன்**—that tells us it’s happening in the present. "I am asking." 

**Analyst Raj (M):** But then Host B said he would do it *later*, after he finished his boxes: **கேட்பேன்**. két-pén.

**Analyst Maya (F):** **கேட்பேன்**. I will ask. No **கு**. Just the future marker. **கேட்குறேன்** vs **கேட்பேன்**. Present vs Future. It’s such a subtle shift, but in a movie plan negotiation, it’s everything!

**Analyst Raj (M):** [Laughing] It really is. And then we had the big "English-origin" verbs that everyone uses: **கேன்சல்** and **போஸ்ட்போன்**.

**Analyst Maya (F):** Yeah, even though they’re English words, the way you use them is so Tamil. To **கேன்சல்** something or to **போஸ்ட்போன்** something to Sunday. It’s perfect "operational capacity" vocabulary.

**Analyst Raj (M):** And Host B was shocked—**நிஜமா?** nija-mā? Really? Like he couldn't believe she'd actually cancel the movie.

**Analyst Maya (F):** And let's not forget the physical work he was doing. He was struggling to **தூக்கு** those boxes. thoo-kku. To lift or carry.

**Analyst Raj (M):** Yeah, you could hear him straining. **தூக்கு**. And then he had to **வை** them on the shelf. vai. To put or place. 

**Analyst Maya (F):** **தூக்கு** and **வை**. Lift and Place. If you can master those two, you can help anyone move house in Coimbatore!

**Analyst Raj (M):** [Laughing] Or at least you can understand when someone tells you where to put the groceries. So, Andrew, pay attention to that **கேட்குறேன்** and **கேட்பேன்** contrast. It’s the key to the future!

**Analyst Maya (F):** Literally! See you all in the next mission.
