# Tier 2 Mission 19: The Homecoming (Breakdown)

**Analyst Maya:** (Teaser) You're about to hear a family arguing over a suitcase. Listen for the word 'small' — it's the one thing they actually agree on. 

[Intercept Plays]

**Analyst Raj:** (Replay Hook) Let's go back... "கீழ இல்ல, மேல வை!" Did you hear that? He's not just complaining, he's giving directions. 

**Analyst Maya:** Welcome back to the breakdown. That was a classic Coimbatore homecoming. Pure chaos! 

**Analyst Raj:** Exactly. Everyone's shouting, everyone's hot, and nobody knows where the bags are. But it's perfect for our spatial grid. 

**Analyst Maya:** Right. Let's look at the first key: **வீடு** (house). And where they are standing: **வாசல்** (entrance/doorway). 

**Analyst Raj:** "வண்டி வாசல்ல நிக்குது" — the car is standing at the entrance. In spoken Tamil, we don't say "வாசலில்". We collapse it to "வாசல்ல". **வாசல்ல**. 

**Analyst Maya:** And then we have the size: **சின்ன** (small). Thambi wants to take the "சின்ன" bag. But where? 

**Analyst Raj:** Akka says "மேல வை" (put it up). Thambi says "கீழ இருக்கு" (it's down). These are two words you've seen before, but they're so easy to flip. **மேல** (Up). **கீழ** (Down). 

**Analyst Maya:** And don't forget **முன்னாடி** (front/before). Akka says "பெட்டிய முன்னாடி வை" (put the box in the front). 

**Analyst Raj:** It's a spatial grid. Up, down, front. **மேல**, **கீழ**, **முன்னாடி**. 

**Analyst Maya:** We also saw a heavy verb today: **பார்த்தேன்** (I saw). Thambi says "நான் பார்த்தேன்" (I saw it). 

**Analyst Raj:** **பார்த்தேன்**. Remember, that "-ேன்" ending tells you *I* did it. **பார்த்தேன்**. 

**Analyst Maya:** Let's do a quick drill. Raj, give them the English, and let's see if they can catch the Tamil. 

**Analyst Raj:** Okay. How do you say... "Small house"? 

[Pause]

**Analyst Maya:** **சின்ன வீடு**. சின்ன வீடு. 

**Analyst Raj:** How about... "Today I saw"? 

[Pause]

**Analyst Maya:** **இன்னைக்கு நான் பார்த்தேன்**. இன்னைக்கு நான் பார்த்தேன். 

**Analyst Raj:** Good. One more. "Go to the house." 

[Pause]

**Analyst Maya:** **வீட்டுக்கு போ**. வீட்டுக்கு போ. 

**Analyst Raj:** Notice how "வீடு" becomes "வீட்டுக்கு" when we say "to the house." It's that flow. 

**Analyst Maya:** This is the "Living Tamil" we've been talking about. It's not "வீடுக்கு," it's "வீட்டுக்கு." It's faster. It's how people actually talk. 

**Analyst Raj:** Alright, you're inside the house now. Put those bags... **மேல** or **கீழ**... and get some coffee. 

**Analyst Maya:** See you in the house!
