MISSION BRIEF: Tier 2, Mission 45

BEAT: A quick lunch at a busy roadside hotel (restaurant) in Coimbatore. The learner is with a friend, comparing two different places. One is bigger (பெரிய), but the other is better (நல்லா இருக்கு). The tension is a friendly debate over which spot has the "proper" (சரியான) sambar. We use the comparison marker (விட) to rank the food, the crowd, and the heat.

NEW:
  - **விட** (*vida*) — Than (comparison marker)
  - **இதை விட அது நல்லா இருக்கு** (*idhai vida adhu nallā irukku*) — That is better than this.
  - **என்னை விட உயரம்** (*ennai vida uyaram*) — Taller than me (introducing the 'vida' pattern for people).
  - **பெரிய** (*periya*) — Big (reinforcing/callback as adjective).
  - **சின்ன** (*chinna*) — Small.

CALLBACKS:
  - **அப்படீங்களா?** (*appaḍīṅgaḷā?*) — Is that so? [never seen in scripts]
  - **கேட்குறேன்** (*kēṭkuṟēn*) — I am asking / hearing [struggled]
  - **கேட்பேன்** (*kēṭpēn*) — I will ask / hear [struggled]
  - **தூக்கு** (*thūkku*) — Lift / Pick up [struggled]
  - **வை** (*vai*) — Keep / Put [struggled]

NOTES:
  - Use 'vida' to compare the heat (sūḍu) of the coffee or the difficulty (kaṣṭam) of finding a seat.
  - Land 'appaḍīṅgaḷā?' as a reaction to a surprising restaurant recommendation.
  - Maintain the street/peer register requested in the profile.
