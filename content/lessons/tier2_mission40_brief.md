# MISSION BRIEF: Tier 2, Mission 40

**BEAT:** The Morning Sprint. You're already late. The house is a whirlwind of last-minute checks. Mama is at the door, comparing your old bag to a new one he found, while you're frantically asking where your keys are. The tension shifts from "I'm late" to "Stop talking about the bag and help me find my things!"

**NEW:**
  - **கிளம்பு** (*Kilambu*) — Leave / Depart (The urgent "Let's go!" or "I'm leaving!")
  - **தேடு** (*Thedu*) — Search / Look for
  - **கிடைச்சுது** (*Kidaichudhu*) — Got it / Found it / Received
  - **சீக்கிரம்** (*Seekiram*) — Quickly / Hurry (Reinforcing as a core routine driver)

**CALLBACKS:**
  - **கேட்குறேன்** (*Kekuren*) — I am asking [struggled]
  - **கேட்பேன்** (*Kep-pen*) — I will ask [struggled]
  - **தூக்கு** (*Thooku*) — Lift / Carry [struggled]
  - **வை** (*Vai*) — Keep / Put / Place [struggled]
  - **நிமிஷம்** (*Nimisham*) — Minute [comfortable/close]
  - **விட** (*Vida*) — Than (Comparison) [comfortable]
  - **புது** (*Pudhu*) — New [comfortable]
  - **பழைய** (*Pazhaiya*) — Old [comfortable]

**NOTES:**
  - Focus on "Social Friction." Mama's curiosity about the "New vs Old" bag (Vida, Pudhu, Pazhaiya) is the obstacle to the learner's "Morning Sprint."
  - Use the "struggled" verbs as the functional needs: "I'm asking (Kekuren) for the keys," "Put (Vai) the bag down," "Lift (Thooku) the box."
