# Tier 2, Mission 48 — The Wedding Secret

## Core Targets
- **Linguistic Pattern:** The Question Chain — யாரு / ஏன் / எப்போ / என்னாச்சு as gossip fuel
- **Scenario Shape:** gossip
- **Location class:** extended_family
- **Energy:** medium-loud

## Scenario Context
The day before a family wedding. Two cousins are helping set up the hall — stacking and moving heavy folding chairs. One of them just found out a secret: a long-absent relative is flying in from Singapore as a surprise. The gossip unspools — who told you, when did you find out, why didn't you tell me, what even happened that made him come back? The question words drive every beat. By the end both cousins know the secret and realise they now have to pretend they don't.

## Word Payload

**NEW (5 words):**
- **யாரு?** (*yaru?*) — Who?
- **ஏன்?** (*yen?*) — Why?
- **எப்போ?** (*eppoo?*) — When?
- **என்னாச்சு?** (*ennaachu?*) — What happened? (fused: என்ன + ஆச்சு)
- **புரிஞ்சுது** (*purinchidhu*) — I understood / Got it (+ negative: புரியல — I don't understand)

**CALLBACKS (4 words):**
- **நிஜமா?** (*nijama?*) — Really? [struggled]
- **கேட்குறேன்** (*ketkureen*) — I am asking/hearing [struggled — verb aspect contrast]
- **கேட்பேன்** (*ketpaen*) — I will ask [struggled — verb aspect contrast]
- **தூக்கு** (*thooku*) — Lift / Carry [struggled — anchor to the physical setup action]

## Notes
- Land கேட்குறேன் / கேட்பேன் as a natural handoff between the two cousins ("I'm asking now" / "I'll ask later") — the Breakdown must replay this contrast clearly.
- என்னாச்சு should be heard as a *fused* natural word, not explained as a compound. Let the Breakdown analysts unpack it.
- தூக்கு should be anchored physically in the scene (actually lifting chairs) so the verb sticks.
- Avoid any kitchen/cooking/restaurant references — well-trodden terrain.
- No morning-rush beats.

## Target Duration
5–7 minutes.
