# Tier 2, Mission 52 — The Maama's Chair

## Core Targets
- **Linguistic Pattern:** The Negation Correction ("appadi illa" as a gentle reframe — not X, but Y)
- **Register:** dread (from Scene Spec)
- **Dramatic Ingredient:** character — the maama is vivid: a man who never raises his voice, whose silence is louder than shouting. His tic: he taps the arm of his chair when he's about to say something important.
- **Scenario Shape:** debrief (Host B tells the story of what happened when the younger brother finally went to the maama)
- **Location class:** extended_family (wedding hall / maama's corner)
- **Energy:** low
- **Episode Form:** story (Host B carries the tale; Host A interjects)

## Scenario Context

Continues M51 "The Empty Chair." At the end of M51, the younger brother walked away to go talk to the maama. This episode is what happened next — told as a story by Host B to Host A, the next morning. The maama was sitting alone in his corner of the hall. When the boy came and started to apologize (wrongly, nervously), the maama stopped him: "appadi illa" — it's not like that. Then quietly told him to sit: "ippa-ve" — right now. Not tomorrow, not later. Right now. The dread is in the waiting — the boy expects anger but gets something worse: patient, gentle correction. The maama's character carries the scene: a man who taps the chair arm, who speaks slowly, who never once raises his voice.
