# Mission 17 Brief: The Overnight Train Call

## Rotation Style: The Surveillance
## Location: Coimbatore-Chennai Express — Sleeper Coach (Moving)

---

## Intel Target
The learner is on the overnight train. The coach is dark, most passengers are asleep. One berth away, a woman is having a hushed, emotionally charged phone call with a family member. She's upset. Something has gone wrong at home. The learner can only hear her side of the conversation, piecing together what happened from fragments. The question words she uses are the keys to understanding the situation.

## The Listenability Test
A found-audio scene of someone having a private crisis on a night train. The eavesdropping tension, the emotional stakes of the call, and the mystery of what happened on the other end make this inherently compelling even without the language learning layer.

---

## Word Status Sheet

### TEACH (The Keys) — 2 words, drilled deeply
- **யாரு?** → Who?
- **ஏன்?** → Why?

### EXPOSE (The Signal) — 10 words, ambient in the Intercept
- **எப்போ?** → When?
- **எப்படி?** → How?
- **கூப்பிடு** → Call (a person)
- **கோவம்** → Anger / Angry
- **கவலை** → Worry
- **கவலைப்படாதீங்க** → Don't worry (respectful)
- **நிஜமா?** → Really?
- **புரியல** → I don't understand
- **என்னாச்சு?** → What happened?
- **வீடு** → House / Home

### USE (mastered — no translation)
- சரி, ஆமா, இல்ல, அவங்க, அண்ணா, அக்கா, மாமா, நேத்து, இன்னைக்கு, அதனால, ஆனா, ஏன்னா, சொன்னாங்க, கேட்டாங்க, முடியாது, முடியும், தெரியும், தெரியாது, இருக்கு, எனக்கு, உனக்கு, அவ, அது, நம்ம, போறேன், வரேன்

### CALLBACK (struggled — embedded in scene)
- **முன்னாடி** (before) — she references something that happened before
- **கிட்ட** (near / to someone) — she's talking about who she spoke to
- **பார்த்தேன்** (I saw) — she describes seeing something

---

## Narrative Arc

### Intercept Beats (3-5 min, ~600 words)
1. **Ambient open**: Train sounds. Coach is quiet. A phone rings softly.
2. **The call begins**: Woman answers. Hushed. "என்னாச்சு?" — What happened?
3. **The escalation**: She's getting details from someone. Uses யாரு? and ஏன்? repeatedly as she tries to understand the situation. Something happened at the வீடு.
4. **The emotional peak**: She gets upset. கோவம் surfaces. She's angry at someone specific. "யாரு சொன்னாங்க?" — Who said that? She argues with the person on the phone.
5. **The resolution attempt**: She calms down. கவலைப்படாதீங்க. She promises to come home. "எப்போ?" she asks. When can she get there? She calculates — the train arrives in the morning.
6. **The quiet ending**: She hangs up. Silence. The train continues.

### Breakdown Beats (6-8 min, ~1000 words)
1. **Cold Open Teaser**: "You're about to hear one side of a phone call on a night train. The woman asks the same question word three times. Listen for it. It's only two syllables."
2. **Replay Hook**: Return to the moment she first says "யாரு சொன்னாங்க?" — the emotional peak.
3. **Unpack யாரு?**: The analysts notice how she uses யாரு? differently each time — first as genuine confusion, then as accusation. "யாரு?" is the Swiss Army knife of Tamil questions.
4. **Unpack ஏன்?**: How ஏன்? lands differently when you already know the answer vs. when you genuinely don't know. The emotional weight of the question word.
5. **Notice the callbacks**: One analyst catches கிட்ட and முன்னாடி woven into her speech — "who did she talk to *before* this call?"
6. **The EXPOSE tease**: Point to கவலைப்படாதீங்க as a "wait, what was that long word?" moment. Don't fully translate — just let the analysts marvel at the structure. One says it, the other breaks the components.
7. **Drill beats**: "Who said that?" → யாரு சொன்னாங்க? "Why?" → ஏன்? Call and response.
8. **Zinger**: "யாரு சொன்னாங்க?" — the perfect phrase for any family gossip situation. Someone tells you something surprising about a relative. Your response: "Who said that?"
