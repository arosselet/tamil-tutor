# Mission Brief: Tier 2, Mission 22 — The Preference Protocol

**Location:** The Family House — Dining Room (Lunchtime).
**Intel Target:** Navigating the "Like/Dislike" minefield of home-cooked food and cinema.

## Word Status Sheet

**TEACH (The Keys):**
- பிடிக்கும் (I like / It pleases)
- பிடிக்காது (I don't like)
- இஷ்டம் (Preference / Liking / Choice)

**EXPOSE (The Signal):**
- என்ன பிடிக்கும்? (What do you like?)
- உனக்கு பிடிக்குமா? (Do you like it?)
- சாப்பாடு (Food)
- படம் (Movie / Picture)

**USE (No Translation):**
- நல்லா இருக்கு (It's good)
- வேணும் / வேண்டாம் (Want / Don't want)
- சாப்பிட்டேன் (Ate)
- கொஞ்சம் (A little)
- சரி / ஆமா / இல்ல (Okay / Yes / No)

**CALLBACK (Struggled/Review):**
- இன்னைக்கு (Today)
- பார்த்தேன் (I saw/watched)
- முதல்ல (First)

---

## Narrative Arc: The Intercept (Discovery)

1. **The Setup:** Andrew is seated for lunch. Amma (Mother-in-Law) is piling rice. Deepa (Wife) is mediating.
2. **The Conflict:** Amma tries to serve *Pavakkai* (Bitter Gourd). Deepa warns her: "He might not like it."
3. **The Pivot:** Andrew uses `பிடிக்காது` for the gourd but pivots to `பிடிக்கும்` for the Chicken Curry to save face.
4. **The Entertainment:** The TV is on in the background. A Rajini movie trailer plays.
5. **The Debate:** Amma asks if Andrew likes Rajini. Andrew has to navigate the "Rajini is God" culture of Coimbatore.

---

## Narrative Arc: The Breakdown (Decryption)

1. **The Cold Open Teaser:** "Listen for the word `பிடிக்கும்`. It’s the sound of you finally having an opinion in this house."
2. **The Replay Hook:** Replay the moment Andrew rejects the bitter gourd. "Did you hear how he softened the blow? He didn't just say 'No', he used the preference pattern."
3. **The Dative Pattern:** Deep dive into `எனக்கு பிடிக்கும்` (To me, it pleases). Explain why we don't say "I like" but "To me, it likes."
4. **The Nuance of 'Ishttam':** Explain `இஷ்டம்`. How it’s used for "Your choice" (`உன் இஷ்டம்`) or personal preference.
5. **The Drill:** Rapid fire. "I like coffee." "I don't like tea." "I like Rajini."
6. **Cultural Context:** The Rajini Factor. Why having a movie preference is a social requirement in Tamil Nadu.
